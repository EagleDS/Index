The Programme

Built for shorter attention spans, not shorter standards.

Two sessions, each standalone. Where the teen programme is a talk followed by a workshop, the junior programme flips the order and the pace — young kids learn by moving, not by sitting still and listening first.

Session One · Hall or Field
Move, Focus, Reset

A fast-moving, games-based session that teaches the physical basics of focus — stopping on a signal, listening under a bit of excitement, and controlling your body when it wants to run wild. No lecture. Kids are moving within the first two minutes.

  • Simple stance, balance, and movement games
  • Listen-and-respond drills that build self-control
  • Partner activities that teach turn-taking and respect
  • Every child included, regardless of fitness or coordination
Session Two · Follow-On (Optional)
Try Again Workshop

Built around one idea: falling down and getting back up is a skill, not a feeling. Kids attempt something physically new, don't get it right away, and try again — with a coach who's spent three decades doing exactly that at the highest level.

  • Foundational striking and movement, adapted for young bodies
  • Structured so no child is singled out or embarrassed
  • A calm-down close so kids leave regulated, not wound up
  • A pathway offered to any child who wants to keep going
What They Take Away

Six things a belt actually teaches.

In martial arts, a belt isn't a prize — it's proof you built something, one piece at a time. That's the model the session runs on, even in a single visit.

Focus

Listening

Coordination
Basic control over a moving body
Confidence

Resilience
Getting it wrong, and trying again anyway
Respect
Working safely with a partner, both ways
Safety & Standards

The question every principal should ask.

Zero
Concussions recorded across 900+ Eagle DS matches and five national tournaments
Combat sports and five-year-olds in the same sentence makes some principals nervous — that's a reasonable instinct, and it's exactly why the programme is built the way it is. There is no sparring, no contact drilling, and no full-contact content of any kind in a junior session. The safety standard is designed and held by Carrie Sanders, a Registered Nurse, and every activity is scaled to what a five-to-twelve-year-old's body and attention span can actually do — not adapted down from a teen or adult curriculum.
