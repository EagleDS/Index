Our Philosophy

The Habits of
Elite People
Start Young.

"The most successful people in the world — in business, in sport, in leadership — share a set of non-negotiable habits. We teach those habits through martial arts."

Every great leader, athlete, and high achiever operates from the same foundation: discipline, pressure management, focus under stress, and a relentless drive for self-improvement.

At Eagle DS, martial arts is the vehicle. The destination is a young person who is technically excellent, mentally strong, and built for whatever comes next — whether that's a national podium, a top university, or running a company.

Your Coach

Dafydd Sanders
"David Eagle"

Founder · Head Coach · NZ Representative · Four Combat Disciplines

There are coaches with experience. There are coaches who have competed at world level. Very few have done both across four completely different combat disciplines.

Dafydd has spent 27 years teaching and coaching across the full spectrum of combat sports — not as a specialist, but as someone who has competed at the highest level in all of them. He represented New Zealand at the IMMAF World Championships 2019 in MMA, the WAKO World Championships in light contact kickboxing (2006) and K1 full contact (2017), and was named Karate All Japan Champion 2010 — an international title with competitors from across the world. In taekwondo he peaked at World #10, competed across two Olympic campaigns, and won the WTF Oceania Championships in 2006. He was named Māori Sports Person of the Year at the University of Auckland in 2005.

That breadth is not incidental — it is the foundation of Eagle DS. When the system covers six styles, sets a contact standard across all of them, and trains coaches and referees to one consistent level, it does so from lived experience inside every discipline. Dafydd's system shapes every class at Eagle DS — delivered by a hand-picked coaching team trained and graded to one standard.

What Dafydd Teaches

1
Technique Before Power

Precision builds the foundation that power is then built upon. The other way produces limits, not athletes.

2
Pressure Reveals Character — and Builds It

Controlled adversity, applied correctly, is the fastest path to mental strength. We don't protect students from discomfort. We teach them to use it.

3
Every Skill Has a Standard

No ambiguity. Students know exactly what they can do, what they can't yet do, and what the next benchmark is.

4
Leaders Are Made in the Dojo

Every advanced student learns to lead. The gym is a leadership laboratory — and the lessons transfer directly to life.

5
The Habits You Build at 10 Run You at 30

This is the most important thing we teach. The highest-performing adults in every field built their operating system early. We're in the business of building that system.
